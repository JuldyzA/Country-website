Kazakhstan

Kazakhstan is famous for its stunning landscapes, offering a wide range of outdoor activities and natural wonders. Its diverse landscapes stretch from the Altai range in the East to the Caspian shores in the West. This vast land is a treasure trove for the adventurous traveller, offering breathtaking beauty right in the heart of Central Asia.

Astana

Kazakhstan's capital city, Nur-Sultan (formerly known as Astana), is a vibrant metropolis located in the northern part of the country. Nur-Sultan is known for its striking modern architecture, featuring futuristic buildings like the Bayterek Tower, which symbolizes the dreams and aspirations of the Kazakh people. The city's layout reflects a blend of traditional Kazakh culture and modern urban planning, with wide boulevards, parks, and public spaces that encourage community engagement. With a diverse population and a growing economy, Nur-Sultan continues to evolve as a dynamic hub in Central Asia, showcasing the rich heritage and ambitious vision of Kazakhstan.

Almaty

Kazakhstan's cultural city is Almaty, formerly the capital and still regarded as the country's cultural heart. Nestled in the foothills of the Trans-Ili Alatau mountains, Almaty is known for its rich history, vibrant arts scene, and diverse population. The city boasts a mix of traditional Kazakh culture and modern influences. Almaty is also home to the renowned Medeo skating rink and the breathtaking Kok Tobe hill, offering panoramic views of the city. Almaty hosts numerous cultural events, festivals, and performances throughout the year, celebrating everything from traditional music and dance to contemporary art. The city's bustling bazaars, like the Green Bazaar, provide a sensory experience of local flavors, crafts, and traditions. With its blend of history, culture, and natural beauty, Almaty continues to be a central hub for arts, education, and cultural exchange in Kazakhstan.
